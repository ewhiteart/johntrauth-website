ABOUT JOHN TRAUTH

John Trauth is a management consultant, strategic planner and author who specializes in transitions. In his books, he helps readers through various self-analysis tools to reveal their motivational needs and then use this information to both plan for and create the future they desire.

Maintaining Optimism While Aging

As we age, doors inevitably close, health challenges arise, loved ones die, and the news – from climate change to foreign wars to divisive politics – seems all bad.  How can we possibly stay happy and optimistic in the face of all this?

We can. And we should!  Scientists have found that lifestyle – how we live our daily life – is by far the major contributor to longevity, not genetics. And the importance of maintaining a positive attitude toward aging has also been clinically proven to extend longevity.  Combining a healthy lifestyle with an optimistic attitude – maintaining confidence and a sense of hope about the future – will positively affect both physical and mental health, help delay disease, and extend longevity.  Yes, we can live happily ever after. And this book will show you how your “ever after” can last even longer.  The essential key: optimism.

"Aging is inevitable—but how we experience it is far more within our control than we once believed. John Trauth explores a powerful and often overlooked truth: Longevity is shaped not only by biology but by how we live—and how we think.

"Drawing from research at the Buck Institute for Research on Aging, Trauth explains how, by combining a healthy lifestyle with optimism, confidence, and a sustained sense of hope about the future, individuals can create a virtuous cycle: better habits, greater resilience, delayed onset of disease, and extended years of vitality. This book offers both a scientific and deeply human perspective on aging—one that replaces fear with agency, and decline with possibility."

—DR. ERIC VERDIN, PRESIDENT AND CEO,
THE BUCK INSTITUTE FOR RESEARCH ON AGING

More about John

John Trauth, MBA is a management consultant based in San Francisco.  He was born in New York, graduated from Colgate University with majors in Economics and Psychology, and proceeded to earn his MBA from the Amos Tuck School at Dartmouth, majoring in marketing, finance and corporate social responsibility.  He has worked for large management consulting firms in both Boston and San Francisco.  For the last 10 years, he has run his own consulting practice specializing in strategic planning.
‍
He and his wife Astrid lived in France in the mid-1990’s, is fluent in French, and now resides in Sausalito, California.
‍
He is co-author, with Alan Bernstein, of “Your Retirement, Your Way,” (McGraw-Hill, 2006) (profiled above) which achieved the #1 position in Amazon’s “retirement book” category in 2007, and later in 2015 was rated by Consumer Reports as one of the 15 best books on retirement.  John and Alan gave keynotes and seminars around the country, counseling people on planning strategically for their retirement.  
‍
In writing “Maintaining Optimism While Aging,” John collaborated again with Alan who provided his perspectives, guidance, and experience as a renowned New York City psychotherapist working with people in major life transitions (school to work, professional transitions, work to retirement and post-retirement living).  Alan is the author of three successful books, “Guide to Your Career” (Princeton Review Corp., 2006) “Mastering the Art of Quitting” (Da Capo Lifelong Books, 2013), and “Your Retirement, Your Way.” (McGraw-Hill, 2006).
